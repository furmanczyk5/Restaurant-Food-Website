Cooking Basics

Cooking is only as complex as you make it. Here are some fundamental techniques used in our recipes.

Pan-Frying

In a shallow, wide pan, heat about a tablespoon or two, just enough to cover the bottom of the pan and keep your food from sticking. When the pan is hot, place the ingredients in the oil. If you wait too long, the oil will start to smoke. Don't use burned oil, but rinse the pan and start over. Frying cooks one side faster than the other, so you may need to turn or stir your ingredients.

Boiling

Fill a tall pot with water and crank up the heat. The water will start to form bubbles. When the bubbles float to the surface pretty uniformly, the water is boiling. Add your ingredients and leave them in for the specified amount of time.

Roasting

Preheat your oven to the specified temperature. This can take a while, so it's best to start preheating before doing anything else. When the oven is preheated, place the ingredients in for the specified amount of time.
